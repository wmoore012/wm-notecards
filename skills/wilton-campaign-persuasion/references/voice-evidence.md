# Voice evidence

Use these source lines as evidence, not as a phrase vending machine. Preserve an exact
line when it belongs to the current argument. Never transplant a joke merely to imitate
the surface voice.

## Notebook and product language

> Hot take: VS Code should tell a HUMAN story.

> I almost failed Machine Learning.

> I was fighting for my life!

> We are data scientists. We make visualizations for a living.

> Why are we still doing machine learning in MS-DOS?

> Jupyter gave me `df.describe()`. I wanted `df.explain()`.

> Excel would NEVER treat us like this and I hate excel.

> Forty columns should not require forty thoughts at once.

> The questions are predictable. The notebook should be too.

> Societies used to record stories on tablets. Now we record them in Jupyter notebooks.

These lines establish a recurring argument supported by several real examples: data
scientists already understand visual communication, but default notebook output often
asks the human to reconstruct hierarchy, memory, and narrative manually.

## Spoken rhythm

Protect short reaction sentences, exact embarrassment, rhetorical questions, and live
self-correction. The thought may break before the grammar would.

Good source behavior:

> WAIT A MIN!

> Nobody can read that.

> That’s part of the job!

> Am I missing something?

Do not manufacture misspellings or profanity. Preserve them only when they carry real
timing or emotional force in the supplied source.

## Pressure through accepted premises

Wilton often states the claim, then uses facts the reader already accepts:

- data visualization improves attention and comprehension;
- executives and students have limited working memory;
- software already knows the data types, missing counts, and distributions;
- good design is normal in consumer and professional software;
- factual integrity still matters more than a beautiful interface.

The contradiction is stronger when the reader supplies the conclusion.

## Fair concessions

Use the strongest fair version:

> of course theres nothing faster than a df.info!

Then show what speed alone fails to prove: a fast diagnostic does not preserve the
question, explain the decision, or help tomorrow's reader reconstruct today's judgment.

## Preattentive emphasis

ALL CAPS mark the emotional peak or the word the reader must see. Do not capitalize the
whole paragraph. Contrast creates the effect.

## Integrity boundary

Separate lived experience, metaphor, evidence, and uncertainty. Strong language does
not authorize unsupported technical claims. Keep computed notebook claims tied to the
actual table, chart, split, and metric.
